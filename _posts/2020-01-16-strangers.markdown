I liked living in the lies brought by life. I loved the illusions of life Daniel would make me believe to be true. I liked believing he loved me. I liked to believe the jewelry he bought me meant something. I liked to believe the weekly flowers were unplanned because he was thinking of me. It made life comfortable and my friends envious. The perfect marriage, they said. 
But he truly didn’t love anyone but himself. 
When they found him, he was lying in a ditch with his secretary. They’d run into a tree and died after flying out of the windshield. It wasn’t a surprise to me. He hated feeling restricted. He didn’t even own belts for that reason. The cops knocked on my door at around midnight. I was already asleep but I woke up with the loud banging. I assumed it was him. But when I opened the door, they took their caps off. I knew immediately that he was gone. And I didn’t cry. I just went into shock. 
Mourning his death was a little strange. A lot of his other mistresses showed up. I knew they were his mistresses because they seemed to be more broken up about everything than I did. I didn’t really feel anything. I was numb and going through the motions of survival. I stood in front of the grave until I was alone, way after the crowds had gone. When it was finally just him and me, I sat down on the grass in front of the dirt pile. 
“Are you okay?” a voice asked behind me. 
I didn’t even turn around. “I…don’t know.”
“Do you need help getting home, Virginia?” Luke asked, approaching my periphery. Luke was Daniel’s best friend and business partner.
“Did you know?” I asked.
He stayed silent for a second. “Please. Let me get you home.” He extended his hand towards me.
I looked at him and took it. He pulled me up gently and put his coat around me. He led me to his car and helped me get into the passenger’s seat. 
“Are you okay?” he asked again once he was in the car, turning on the ignition.
“Are you?”
He hesitated. “I don’t know. I just…feel pretty damn awful about what happened to him. He called me before he got on the wheel. I just didn’t pick up. I went to bed.”
“I was asleep, too.” I looked at him. “How long was Daniel cheating on me?”
“Would it change anything?”
“No. His image is shattered to me already. But I want to know. Was it always that way?”
“Daniel…” he started, “he tried really hard to be faithful. He did love you.”
“There’s something in those words that doesn’t make sense.” 
“He knew how you felt about cheating. He didn’t want to lose you.”
“He cheated on me the whole time,” I stated.
“Yes.”
I stayed silent. Instead I watched the cars pass by us and people walking along the sidewalks. I wished I was them. They seemed so content. 
“I’m sorry. I wanted to tell you. But I figured the reasons were more selfish than anything so I didn’t,” he said.
“Selfish?”
“That’s a story for another day, Tory,” he said. 
We continued the silence until we got back to the house I used to share with Daniel. 
“So what now?” he asked after parking.
“I need a full time job. Daniel refused to allow me to work in anything permanent. He would just completely go ballistic…I should probably start with that.”
“Why don’t you take over his position?”
I frowned. “How am I supposed to do that?” 
“You spent some time at the office. I can show you the ropes until you get the hang of it fully. Just because he screwed you over doesn’t mean you can’t take advantage of this opportunity now. That is…if you want it.”
I looked at Luke, who I was friends with before I met Daniel. He’d introduced us actually. He was genuine in his offer. He’d remained single for a very long time…And it dawned on me. Selfish reasons indeed.
Life was too complicated to deal with that right now.
“I’ll think about it. Thank you, Luke,” I said before I headed out of the car.  
“If you need anything, call me,” he said. “I mean it.”
“I know.” I waved as I closed his door and went inside the house I shared with a departed stranger. I didn’t know him. I didn’t even really know Luke given what I’d just learned. But I did need a livelihood. This was the time when I needed to take the bull by the horns. 
I pulled out my phone. 
Me: I’m in.
Luke: See you Monday
