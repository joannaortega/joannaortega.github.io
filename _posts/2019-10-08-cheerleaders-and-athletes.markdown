Romero was a very athletic soccer player. Everyone liked him; he was kind and really tried really hard at things he wasn’t good at.He put the time and effort into things that didn’t come naturally easy to him and he was never afraid to ask questions or for help. He was endearing to all. And I was one of those people that was really endeared by him. He had a goofy grin and messy hair. He was very brave. He took a chance and didn’t let anything keep him away from reaching what he wanted to reach. 

But even through this all, I was not in love with him. I didn’t get the butterflies in my stomach when he looked at me or talked to me. I didn’t feel the insanity or need to have him in my life. Don’t get me wrong; I very much liked him. I thought he was a great person. I just wasn’t in love.

There was someone that did give me butterflies. It was the cheerleader that couldn’t stop looking at him. Her name was Camila. She usually had a small ponytail while she wore her burgundy and black and white uniform. She was so pretty. She had freckles underneath her makeup and green eyes. She made sense. She was perfect. She was gorgeous. 

But she looked at Romero the way I looked at her. I noticed these things, unfortunately. And I wished she would like me back. But I had Romero and that wasn’t fair. 

As I was wrapped in my own world, I bumped into her in the hallway. 

“Oh, dear God. I’m sorry,” I told her.

“It’s fine,” she said, refusing to meet my eyes.

“You know, I know you like Romero,” I told her.

She looked at me. Her eyes widened. “Listen, I never made a move on him.”

“I know. But you will because I don’t think we will be together for too much longer.”

“Why?”

“Because I like you.”

Her eyes widened even more. She’s got the biggest eyes I’ve ever seen.

I smiled at her a bit. “Good luck.” 

I walked away.
