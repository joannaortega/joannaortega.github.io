“Have you ever been kissed in the rain?” I asked Luke, my best friend, as we watched The Notebook on TV.

“Nope,” he said, his lips popping. “But I’ve kissed people in the rain.”

“What’s the difference?” I asked him.

“I initiated the kiss every time.”

“Were you not kissed back?”

He paused. “I wanted to be a smartass, Nova. It backfired.”

I laughed. “Is it nice?”

“Sure. It is very romantic.” 

I nodded to myself. “I just figured it’d be the spitting out of water…or the swallowing of a lot of it.” I wrinkled my nose. I lifted up my water to my lips and imagined trying to kiss someone through that. I swallowed before Luke could see how stupid I could look.

“Let’s try it.”

“Wait. What?” I asked, almost spitting the liquid remnants out of my mouth.

“Let’s try it. In the name of science.”

“Are you serious?”

“I’m not serious but I’m willing.”

“Okay,” I said slowly. “Fine. Next time it rains, let’s do it. For science.”

---

At the first storm, I heard a knock on my door. After opening my door, I saw Luke standing there in the pouring rain, with athletic wear on. 

“Jesus, you’re going to get sick,” I told him. “Come in!”

“You come out,” he said and pulled my shoulders. 

I remembered our conversation. We walked more towards the heavier rain. 

“Open eyes?” I asked. 

“Whatever you want.”

He cupped my chin. I felt something turn in my stomach. I reached up to put my hands on his cheeks. 

“God, you’re cold,” I said as I touched him.  

When he leaned towards kissing me, hair from his swooped, gelled hair went straight into my eye. 

“Oh fuck, gah. This is so stupid,” I complained, turning my head down to get the water out.

“Oh, just close your eyes, you dork.” 

I did and faced up. “You’re the dor-” 

His mouth was on mine. At first, it was a small peck and pressing of the lips for a second. But then, our bodies got close to one another. I wrapped my arms around his neck and pulled him down to my lips again. His arms wrapped around my waist, picking up the intensity. A sharp thunder strike a couple of seconds later finally led us to stop. 

 “Do you want to come in? It’s probably not a good idea for you to be outside in the rain so much. I have tea,” I asked him. I felt a slight blush on my face. 

He nodded. “Okay,” he said, his voice rough and a deep intensity in his eyes. 

He took my hand and we slowly walked inside.
