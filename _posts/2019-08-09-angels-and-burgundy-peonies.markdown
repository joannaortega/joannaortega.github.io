I walked around the nursery, looking for my favorite flowers: snapdragons, roses, and some peonies. Like my grandmother, grandfather, and (living) father, we all reached an age where we appreciated flowers. Me, I spent so much time not living life that I needed life around me. Unfortunately for me, they weren’t beings that could talk, and I lived alone in states away from my family for college. So here I was, in a floral shirt, searching for the perfect complement to the balcony in my apartment.

I wanted a burgundy or a pastel pink flower, both favorite colors along with black. Unfortunately there weren’t any black flowers near me. So I walked around, in search of the flower that would fit the perfect picture I had in my head. I walked past some daffodils, bright yellow like the sun. They were so beautiful. I walked away, hesitant because I loved the flower, and although it wasn’t anywhere near my favorite color, I felt quite fond of the little beak it seemed to have, kind of like a duck. I walked past the tulips, which grew there in an array of flowers, beautiful and unique on its own. There were ferns and grasses and rainbows around me. I kinda wanted to get some appropriately colored azaleas but man, they were a lot just in general and they weren’t really my kind of thing. They seemed like a very delicate flower. I needed mine to at least attempt to be a little bit livelier or resilient to life. 

As I searched in the distance, I saw some shades of pastel in the distance. I started rushing over and saw that they were the peonies I’d been looking for in that pastel pink and a beautiful peach. There were a couple of burgundies at the end of the lane. As I picked up my pace, I felt something hold my leg and wrap around. I started to fall and tried to use my arm to try to hold on to something but the last thing I felt was water on my face as it hit the pavement. 

“Hello? Ma’am?” A young man with hair down to his shoulders called out at me. He was very cute and the sunlight wrapped around him like a halo.

“Holy fuck. Are you an angel?”

“Close, that’s my name,” he said with a chuckle. “Do you remember yours?” 

“Itzel. I’m 21, a senior in college. It’s 2019. I came here for peonies and now I really want to go home.” I tried to sit up, and he helped me. 

“Well, humor me, Itzel. I just saw you fall and hit your head and as a doctor, I can’t let you go like this.” He turned on the flashlight on his phone. “Look straight at me.” He flashed the light in my eyes.

“Well, your eyes are constricting,” he said. “How do you feel?”

“My head hurts a little bit and my arm a little bit. But I’m fine.” My arm was bleeding a little. 

“Any ringing in your ears? Nausea?”

“No,” I said and smiled. “But thank you for all of this.” I tried to get up but he held me where I was.

“Can you please wait? I’m not finished.”

“Well, pardon my french, but my bum is in the water, and I’m getting cold.”

“Oh, right. I’m sorry.” He helped me up. “Let me see your arm.” 

He tested the range of motion and I flinched a little bit. “Nothing seems to be broken, but maybe you should go to the hospital.”

“Oh, no. It’s honestly not as bad as it seems. It’ll probably just get red and bruise up, but I don’t swell easy so it’s fine.”

He snorted. “That’s not really an answer.”

“Well, if it gets really serious, then I’ll get it checked out.”

“Okay. Well, if the pain gets worse, there may be a fracture. Listen, I would really rather you get this checked out.”

“I promise, scout’s honor.” 

“You were a girl scout?” he asked.

“Nope. But the message is conveyed, is it not?”

He smiled. “Alright.”

“Well, thank you for all of this. I will be leaving now.” I turned to leave after granting him a small wave. I headed to my car and as I started the ignition, I saw him approach my window.

“Wait, you’re driving?” he asked.

“Yes, why?”

“You just hit your head.”

“You said it wasn’t a concussion.”

“Do you mind if I follow you home to make sure you get there safely?”

I hesitated. I looked at his face and there was nothing but concern there. “Oh...okay. Yeah. That’s fine.”

He nodded and headed to his car. I drove out and he followed me until I got to my apartment. 

I parked and pointed at my building. “This is me.”

“Still feeling okay?” 

“Yep. Thanks, Angel.”

“No problem, Itzel. Get some rest, okay?”

I nodded and waved goodbye. I went up and into my apartment. I went straight to shower and clean up my scrapes. When I got done, I realized I didn’t lock my car. 

I tried to run outside and I almost fell to burgundy peonies on my doorstep.

I picked them up and looked at them and a card. 

>The thought of you not having these flowers after literally falling for them kinda destroyed me. If you ever want to have dinner… -Angel

He attached his number. 

I locked my car and took my flowers inside.
