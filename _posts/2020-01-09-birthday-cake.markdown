I loved baking. Pies, cake, brownies, cookies, you name it. There was something nice about simply following a recipe and just entertaining your mind with that. I loved the Great British Baking Show and I was happy to be a part of a community in this way. So whenever my friends had a birthday, I would bake them a cake. 
This time around was my friend Alexei. He was honestly one of the best people I know. He laughs with a little chuckle and then a very endearing snort. He was such a gorgeous person. He was honestly the sweetest person you could meet. He was always so supportive of me in work and out. He was doing random acts of kindness that no one ever really noticed. He got up really early for a week just so we could carpool to work when my car was in the auto shop. And like his personality, he was really into desserts and sweets. He liked red velvet the most, so I decided to make him a red velvet cake with chocolate chips and some cream cheese frosting. 
He was supposed to come by today in the evening, so I mixed the batter really early this morning. I wanted to make sure the cakes had enough time to cool in this insanely hot day. I wanted to bake before the sun was high up and I would need to run up my electric bill. He was totally worth it though.
I was just finishing the frosting when I heard a knock on the door. Alexei was early, which I could have guessed. He was always early to everything. 
I smiled. “Come in, friendo.” 
He walked in. “Thanks,” he said. 
“I just got your cake done. Sorry. Haven’t had much time to get the place tidy.”
He laughed. “You’ve seen my place. Trust me, you’re fine.”
“Do you want to go out for dinner and then come back for cake?”
“Can we just eat cake?”
“I mean…it’s your birthday. We can do whatever the heck you want.”
“I’ve been waiting for this all year. I’m ready for a slice of cake.” 
I led him to the kitchen where I had the layered cake already ready. I lid a candle on top and sang a very soft and basic version of happy birthday. I couldn’t sing. I wasn’t going to subject him to too much of my voice. He closed his eyes when I was finished and after a couple of seconds, he blew out the candle. 
“Whatcha wish for?” I asked. 
His cheeks got red. “Can’t tell you.”
“Oh, come on. I’ll help you make it come true.”
He closed his eyes before taking a deep breath. When he opened his eyes, he looked at me intently before cupping my face and lowering his lips to mine. After the initial shock, I melted in his arms.
“Happy birthday,” I whispered.
“Indeed.”
