Pebbles hit my window, softly, a tap tap tap like rain. I was studying in silence at my desk, trying to cram 15 pages of notes into my brain. I turn on the radio at a reasonable volume, not enough to elicit a reaction from my parents but enough so they won’t hear anything going on outside. I open the window and lean out to see him, Eleazar. I smile, surprised by his appearance.

“Can I come up?” he asks. His hair is a bit matted from the sweat. He still has his jersey on. He probably just came from football practice.

I felt much better about my hair being in a messy bun and the big hoodie I had on. Not that this was super out of the norm; it seemed that I was always a mess. On good days when I needed to look put together, I put on a simple dress or a simple shirt and jeans. I thought I should be used to him seeing me like this by now.  

“Depends on what you want,” I reply, knowing my cheeks are warming up. 

He starts climbing up. “I only want to talk, Adelaide.” 

He’s a very athletic guy. He was sporty and a running back in football. Within a couple of seconds he is in my room. I run to lock the door, silently. He opens his arms for an embrace which I quickly run into. 

“I’m a good boy, remember,” he whispers. 

“Good boys don’t sneak into a girl’s room ever,” I whisper back to him. 

“Adelaide, look at me.”

I look up to meet his warm brown eyes. I move my arms from around his waist to reach and loop over his shoulders the best I could. He smiles at me and lowers his face. I lift the heels of my feet to meet his lips. He is quite gentle at first and then picks up the intensity a little before kissing my forehead. 

“You know I have feelings for you. And….I think you have feelings for me,” he says, with an intense look in his eyes. “Will you...will you be my girlfriend?”

I smile. “Yes.” 

He grins wide and kisses me again. He looks very excited. “Can I pick you up tomorrow?” he asks.

I nod because that’s all I really know how to do. I don’t really know how to think now. He was so beautiful. I caress his cheek and reach up to plant a kiss on his cheek. He turns his face to get another kiss before we both know he has to leave.

“We also have a game on Friday. Will you be there?” he asks as he walked towards the window. 

“Aren’t I always?” 

He smiles and presses a kiss to the back of my hand. “I’ll be here bright and early.” 

He leaps out of the window and quickly down the ledge. He smiles and waves before running off. 

I melt on the floor.
