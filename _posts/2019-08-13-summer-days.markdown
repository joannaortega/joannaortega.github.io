When I met her, she had pigtails on. She was on the monkey bars, higher above the ground than her height, or so I thought at the time. When you’re five, everything seems huge and everyone seems old. But not Millie. She was smaller than I was and young and careless. Straight after that, she ran to the swings near my teenage brother and his friends. She just started swinging around. Even I didn’t get near my oldest brother when he was with his friends.

Now here she was, more than a decade later, diving off the cliff and into the deep end. Millie was always braver than I was. I didn’t trust the water, any large body of water, really. I nearly drowned once in a community pool and she saved me with her tiny body, nearly drowning herself. But she got right back in back then. And I never stepped foot in again. I just sat in the sidelines because she didn’t like to leave me alone nor to be alone. I was working through a series of science fiction this summer as she swam around.  

“Ginoooooooooo,” she called out from the edge of the pool.

“Yes, Miss Millie?” I said, properly, as if I was her butler because I knew that would make her laugh.

And she did, without fail, like she would always laugh. “So, why don’t you dip your toes in?”

“You know why.”

“Gino, you are tall enough for the five foot end. By like over a foot.”

“Milagros, don’t push it.” I was the only person allowed to call her Millie, but when I meant business, I used her full name. Which would prompt her to use my full name, which of course, everyone but her used. 

“Gianno.” I knew it. 

“Please.” 

She got out of the pool and walked towards me. I frowned. She had the temper of a firecracker. 

“What are you doing?” I asked her. 

She twisted the top of her hair in my direction to soak me with the water she was squeezing out. Millie sat on top of me, leaning back as if I was a cushion on top of the lounge chair. I set my book down, trying to keep it dry. 

“What are you doing?” I repeated.

“I’m shielding you from the sun. You’re gonna burn like leather. Besides, you should smell like a pool after going to one.”

“I have Italian blood; I tan. And you’re getting me wet.” 

She snorted. “You would complain about getting wet when going to a pool.”

“You know why I’m here.”

“So we can hang out. But we hardly hang, sweetie. Especially not when I’m just over there by the pool and you’re just here reading.”

“It is what it is.”

She flipped over so our noses were pressing together. 

I don’t know why I did the next thing I did, but I pressed my mouth to hers. 

And she kissed me back. 

And I couldn’t think of anything else other than wanting to keep doing so. There was this beautiful woman here, in my arms. I pulled her body closer to me. And I felt a deep ache in my chest for her. 

“Come in the pool with me,” she whispered to me. “I won’t let you fall.”

“Okay.” 

She stood up slowly and extended her hand to me. I took it with an iron grip, and we stepped slowly into the pool on the 3 foot end. She held me tightly around my waist with her tiny bikinied body. And yet, I felt safe because although the water barely touched my hips, she wasn’t letting go. 

“Are you feeling okay here?” she asked.

“Yeah. I’m okay.”

“Good,” she replied before pulling me down for another kiss.
