“Girls day,” Sienna told me. “Come on, we haven’t done this in a very long time.” 

“Fine. But I have a date with Sebastian at 7, so we need to be done by then,” I replied as I got into her car.

“What’s the occasion?”

“Oh, it’s our third anniversary.”

“Wow, it really has been that long, huh? What are you wearing tonight by the way?”

“Ohh… I don’t know.”

“Maybe you should wear the red dress you have. The lacy one. It looks nice on you.”

“Hey, thanks, I hadn’t really thought about it. Work has sucked recently.”

“Do you want to match your nails to the dress?”

“I guess so. Are we doing nails?”

“That and a spa day if you’re okay with it.”

“I’m good with that.”

“Cool!”

---
That evening, Sebastian picked me up at around 6:30. I wore the dress Sienna suggested with some heels. He wore a button down shirt that matched the color of my dress. 

“You look beautiful, Charlotte,” Sebastian said.

“Thanks, cutie,” I replied with a wink. “You’re not so bad yourself.”

He laughed. “So, after dinner, I was thinking we could go walk in the park.”

“If you’d like, we can do that. I’m flexible, hun.”

He smiled. “Well, I’m considering the farfalle pasta with beef.”

“Me too. But with the chicken.”

He smiled. 

We ordered our food and drank some wine. We talked. He seemed jitterier than normal, but I didn’t want to ask. 

---

Afterwards, we went to the park, to our spot. We had a park bench where we met while walking our dogs.

We sat down and he leaned in to kiss me. I happily leaned in to it. 

He pulled away and got down on his knee.

Oh god.

His lips were moving but I couldn’t hear. He pulled out a ring.

“Will you marry me?” he asked.

I nodded, my eyes welling up. We kissed each other so much, and we were both sniffling. The ring was beautiful and simple. Very me. I looked at my hand and was reminded to thank Sienna. My nails were perfect.
