I loved violent storms. I know that they caused big destruction for many people, but I slept the most peaceful whenever it rained heavily with thunder. If there was some wind, it was even better. If it rained during the day, I spent the time writing when the sky was dark and gray. Sometimes I would play a record. I lived alone in my small house. It was nice and sturdy and had enough space for my solitary life. It was easy to keep clean and organized. I lived a simple life with simple pleasures: nice records, some candy, some nice comfortable clothes, nice fruits and vegetables from my trees and plants in my backyard. I only went out when I desperately needed to or wanted to. And I preferred it that way. 

Luckily for me, tonight was one of those nights. I played an old record while the sky began to get gray. I made sure nothing important was on the floor in case of flooding. Not that it ever flooded in my home, I had a small lake in the backyard where water tended to pool into. But I liked being safe. I put the potted plants in my front patio to give them a fighting chance at survival. They would get rain water but they probably wouldn’t deal with too much of the wind.  

I walked back inside and sat on my chair. I opened my blinds and sat down at my desk. I lit my 3-wick eucalyptus and mint candle and began to write. Two hours later, I heard something fall when the wind was particularly strong. 

I put on my rainboots and my really large rain jacket. One of my plants fell from their hanger on my porch. I went out and tried to get the soil back into the cracked pot and placed the whole thing next to the door to try to keep it from dying and going into shock from further environmental weather. I brought down the other pot that was still hanging aggressively from the patio ceiling.

I heard some whining from the patio area. I pulled back my hood, so I could look from my peripheral video. I couldn’t see anything. Water splattered more on my face. I stepped out of my patio. I walked to look under the car. There was nothing there other than some weeds. I ran to my backyard but I didn’t hear anything anymore. I ran around the lake, looking in and trying to figure out if there was anything there. I walked back to the front of my house and looked around. 

I didn’t find anything. My face was so wet. I was cold. And I didn’t hear anything anymore. I moved to step back inside. I heard a loud howl echo. 

That echo...I’d heard it below my patio before. I slid below to see a muddy dark thing. It was afraid of the water and the puddles by the way it was trying to avoid getting wet even though it looked to be caked in mud. 

“Come here,” I crooned. “It’s okay, honey.”

I began to sing ‘Somebody to Love’ by Queen, softly. It started walking towards me, whatever it was. I approached it, slowly, extending my arms. It came to me slowly and I picked up the small four-legged creature. I ran into the patio and set him down before getting my boots off. It shook its fur. It was a puppy underneath the mud. I wrapped him in the rain jacket and took him to the sink. I washed him as quickly as I could because he was scared of the water. He was beautiful. 

I fed him some peanut butter and dry popcorn, which he voraciously ate. As I left him and his dinner in my kitchen, I went to go shower. As I showered, he popped his head in, his tail wagging. 

I set up some pillows on the floor, and a blanket. I placed him in the center of it. He snuggled around. 

I was exhausted. After turning off the light, I went to bed. I heard a creak in my door a few seconds later. The puppy barked a little and I pulled him on the bed. 

“Don’t pee here,” I ordered. 

He snuggled under my arm. And with that, he had my heart.

“Alright, alright. Good night.”
