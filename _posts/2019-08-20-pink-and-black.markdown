Sofia laid in her bed, listening to “With or Without You” by U2. She had enough breakups where she had a breakup playlist. She knew afterwards was “Separate Ways” by Journey. After that, she was 85% sure it was “November Rain” by Guns and Roses.

She took her music very seriously and refused to listen to Taylor Swift. Sofia would dabble with chick flicks if she was in a mood but otherwise would just deal with breakups the way she always did, by stuffing her face with chips, chocolate, and ice cream, singing very loudly, and crying until she couldn’t cry any more. 

She knew she would be fine. But for now, it just hurt. For now, it felt like part of her chest had been ripped out by someone with long sharp claws. There was no animosity between her and Fernando. They thought they would marry. But he got the opportunity of a lifetime: a job abroad in Spain. He couldn’t say no, and she couldn’t go with him. They both cried at the realization together. They had a last dinner. They sealed their relationship with a kiss and promise that they would talk again when it hurt less. 

It wasn’t that day yet. 

She hugged the sweater he let her have and smelled his scent. It didn’t soothe the ache but it made her feel better. 

Two songs passed when she heard a knock on the door. She didn’t remember ordering food but got up anyway, wrapping herself in her blanket to cover up her dirty clothes. 

“It’s time to go out,” her sister, Sara, said as she yanked the door completely open and walked right in. 

“Sara, please don’t.” Sofia frowned. 

“No, Sofia. You are in a funk. We are going out,” Sara said before yanking the blanket from her sister. “But first, shower, woman!”

Sofia went back to the couch to try to ignore Sara and drown her out. Sara started pulling on her sister. “Sof, get your butt up. I’m not playing with you.”

“Listen, honey, maybe you don’t get it. This meant something. And I loved him. And he was my best friend. And now he’s gone and I can’t hate him for that reason because if the roles were reversed, we would have chosen the same for me.” 

“I understand that, Crazy,” she murmured. “But I need you to get clean and I need you to come out into the sunlight for a couple of hours. Let’s go get ice cream at the mall, okay?” 

Sofia sat up and considered it. “Are you paying?”

“Yes. Go get ready.” 

“Fine.” She got up and went to shower. 

She had to double shampoo because she’d gotten a lot of gunk into her hair when she was eating and spilling and crying. Also, it was oily from the lack of showering. She cleaned herself up the best she could, until her skin was red and raw. She went slowly through her old after shower routine. She moisturized with lotion and witch hazel. Sometimes coconut oil. Fernando would love the combination of smell on her skin. But today she decided to do this for herself. She wrapped herself in a robe and put her clothes on slowly. It was her favorite shirt--The Beatles--and favorite shorts--high waisted and black. She wore her combat boots. She combed through her tangled hair and decided to avoid makeup for now. 

Sofia walked out of her room to meet Sara on the couch. 

“Ready?” Sara asked.

“Yeah. Let’s go.”

As they got into her car, Sofia’s phone buzzed. 

>Fernando: It doesn’t hurt less. But I miss you.

Sofia smiled at her phone and watched as they rode into a highway and past some traffic. She wasn’t the only one dying. 

And breakups were hard.

She looked at her sister, older, wiser, and pink. Sara had become quite the woman. 

It could be worse.
