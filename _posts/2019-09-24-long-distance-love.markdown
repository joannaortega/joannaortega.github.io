I hadn’t seen him in a very long time. Everyone told us that long distance relationships don’t work. But somehow, we had made it through another year leading our count to six years together. We were still close, we Skyped often and texted everyday. I loved him so much. He was my favorite person in the whole wide world.

We knew each other since we were in middle school. We started off as friends, sitting next to each other for two years. We started dating in high school after we both joined the soccer team my sophomore year of high school, me as a goalie and him as a forward. We were both good so we tried to practice together. Then, in one of those moments, he approached me with the ball. I tried to block him and he tried to get the ball in. We both ended up on the floor, him on top of me. We looked at each other and then we kissed. His lips were very soft, and he tasted like grape popsicle. He blushed and so did I. We confessed our feelings to one another. We’d been together ever since. 

After graduation, we went to separate colleges. He went out of state across the country on a full ride scholarship while I stayed in our town with a partial scholarship. It was the cheapest university I could attend. Despite the distance, we didn’t want to break up. We’d already been through so much. And it was hard sometimes. Our majors were difficult, and we barely got to see each other. We were always busy. But we loved each other. 

So now here I was, holding up my I-Pad with his name, Jake, visible on a slide and some flowers on my other hand. I waited for him to arrive to the airport.

Then, suddenly, I made eye contact with him. He looked tired but when he saw me, he started running towards the gate. I ran to meet him, throwing myself into his arms and pressing my lips to his. 

“Hi, Jake,” I whispered to him. 

“Hi, Charlie,” he whispered back. 

We held each other like that for a second. It had been too long. Tears filled my eyes pretty quickly. His did too. 

We both laughed and kissed again. 

“It’s been too long,” he said to me. 

“Yeah. I brought you these flowers.”

“They are really nice. Thanks, babe,” he said and took them as I picked up his suitcase. 

We walked to the car. 

“Six years, babe,” I told him as we got into our seats. 

“Yep.” He took my hand and kissed the back of it. His eyes focused on my hand. “Let’s go home!”

I started the car. We were ready to go build our home together.
