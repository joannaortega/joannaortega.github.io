You don’t choose your family, they say. It’s true. You can choose your friends. You can consider them family. But you can’t choose genetics. And sometimes you just have to accept that they love you and you love them for whatever they are and whoever they are. I’m sure there are grey areas like those that are unaccepting of certain lifestyles and murderers.
Like my little sister. She was amazingly annoying and confusing. She was a walking contradiction. She just felt everything so passionately, it was overwhelming to be around her. She sucked the energy out of me. I coasted in the middle, not understanding why her anger was so explosive or why her love was so deep. Every time I felt sick or upset, she would stay with me overnight, just hugging me and telling me things would be okay. And she was a lot. But she always meant well. 
I couldn’t even bring myself to be upset with her due to her outbursts; her emotions have always been genuine. She was that rowdy and unruly as a baby and toddler. Growing up, I’d heard and seen the constant slamming of doors, the pitchy celebrations, and the heartbreaking tears. 
And now she was here, dressed in a pale pink blushy dress, just waiting for the moment where she would no longer officially be my problem and be my new brother-in-law’s. She kept having to blink back tears, holding my hand as she sat patiently waiting when she was officially ready and dressed. 
She was much braver than I was. 
“Are you ready, Alicia?” I asked her through the chaos all around us. 
“Very.” 
“How?” I asked, feeling insanely puzzled that she wasn’t freaking out. 
She shrugged. “I love Edgar, Silvia. It’s that simple. No cold feet. No doubts. Just ready for life with him.”
I shook my head, closing my eyes.  “I don’t know how you do it.”
“Do what?” 
“Feel all the time…so intensely.”
Her lips curled up. “It’s a lot. But it’s the only way I know to be.”
“And Edgar knows how to deal with you when you get in a mood which is great.”
She snorted. “Much better than you could.”
Yes, he did. He very much loved her and had the patience of a saint.
The wedding planner came into the room. “Alright, ladies. Time to rock and roll.”
We stood up. 
Before I let go of her hands to get in position, I said to her, “I love you, Alicia. No matter what.”
“Love you, too.” I saw tears start to pool her eyes. 
I took a small bouquet and walked in front of her, leading her to the rest of her life as we went to the garden for her ceremony.
