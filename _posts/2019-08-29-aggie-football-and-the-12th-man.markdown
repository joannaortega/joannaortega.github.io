I was applying my mascara on my eyelashes when I got a text message. 

>Margaret: Hey! I’m downstairs! 

I smiled and quickly dabbed a bit of maroon lipstick on my lips, hoping to avoid too much smearing of color. I looked in the mirror, making sure nothing was terribly out of place. I felt cute in my maroon romper. Alright. I pulled some water bottles from the freezer and my 12th Man towel. I ran out the door and downstairs to meet Margaret.

She was wearing a maroon tank top and some shorts with some athletic shoes. I handed her a water bottle which she immediately wrapped with her towel from the cold temperature. 

“Ready?” I asked, already knowing the answer.

She smiled. “Yep.”

We made our way to Kyle Field, just us two. It was our final year in college and neither of us wanted to play around with our Aggie football attendance. 

“How did your paper go this week?” I asked her as we walked.

“It was fine. I read way too much to get through it. I’ll get the grade next week.”

I smiled. “Isn’t that the way with liberal arts?” 

We both knew that to be true. She was a History major and I was an English major. We always had papers due, more often than tests which I preferred. 

“But now, football!” she proclaimed. We crossed the train tracks and crossed the two lanes to get to the stadium. 

We loved college football. Mine was born from my brother talking about it and the sense of belonging to something bigger than myself. For Margaret, it was definitely because her dad was old alumni. Man, it was fun. We yelled along with our fellow Aggies at the instruction of our Yell Leaders. We swayed together. And we all collectively got to de-stress from the frustrations daily school load together. We stood for four hours to collectively tell our team, we are here and we aren’t going anywhere. We are ready for when you need us. And then, when their offensive team comes on the field, we yell louder. 

There is nothing quite like that rush, the lightheaded feeling that comes when you can’t quite breathe because your lungs and vocal cords are exerting themselves further than you normally do for daily life. 

We went to our seats, hoping the sun would set sooner rather than later. The game was starting in 20 minutes at 6:00 pm. We were grateful for the evening games. Most daytime games left me sunburnt; there wasn’t enough sunscreen I could put on that would stop the redness from coming in. But I couldn’t leave no matter how hot it got--we couldn’t leave. There were only so many games I would get to see. I needed to make the most of it. 

“So glad we get to do this together for our final season,” I murmured to her.

“Same here, Gen. We should get Chills after half time.” 

“Totally.”

We always did wait to get my strawberry and her lemon Chill Italian Ice. And we always waited after half time because we weren’t about to miss the Nationally Famous Fighting Texas Aggie Band. Lines were always so long anyway. But they were worth it. The whole experience was worth it.

We eventually stood up when the game nearly began. They started to play the anthem and a starting reel. Power by Kanye West started playing. We looked at each other. 

“It’s time!!!” we said in unison with the rest of the crowd watching with the rest of the proceedings. 

We were seniors ready to Whoop. We earned our Whoop. And we were ready. 

It was time for football.
