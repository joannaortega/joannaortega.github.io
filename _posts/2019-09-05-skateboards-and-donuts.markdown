I always stop to smell the roses. I believe the Italians when they say that Americans are always running and always in a rush. When I see people around while I’m downtown, they are all always speeding around, beelining towards their destination. I understood that people were sometimes in a rush. Sometimes people were trying to make things on time. And I totally understood that. But people didn’t stop to take a breath when they weren’t in a rush or on a priority deadline. If people didn’t slow down, life would very much pass them by without them realizing. 

I was more of a journey type of girl. I loved looking at all of the stores. A lot of them were bars but there were small stores here and there. I smelled very deliciously sweet smells amidst the trash around. It wasn’t the nicest area but they had some great places. I went into the place, Voodoo Donuts. I got a yeast donut with Oreo cookies, chocolate, and peanut butter to treat myself, and I decided to continue walking. 

It was delicious and amazing. I loved it. I spilled some cookie crumbs which made me a bit sad, but it was a delicious donut. No regrets. I continued walking and saw a shirt selling some little trinkets. I saw a little rainbow colored unicorn and stopped to look. 

I admit, I was careless. I didn’t look to see who was behind me. I felt a giant force throw me against the window of the store. I dropped my donut and fell on the floor. 

I groaned from the pain and the startling event. I fell on my arm. I saw this girl around me on the ground. She was on her back and I could immediately see her long blonde hair. She got up immediately and ran to pick up her skateboard. She ran back as I was getting up. 

“Hey, I am so sorry!” she exclaimed, her green eyes wide. 

“It’s okay! I stopped suddenly,” I replied, flattening my skirt, which was now dirty on the side. My arm was scraped and it broke the skin a little bit.

“You’re bleeding!” she said, reaching out to me. 

I gave her a small smile. “Yeah, that’s okay though.”

She dragged me into a store and into their bathroom. She started to clean the wound with soap and water. “I’m sorry. I really should have been more careful. But the street was busy so I didn’t wanna try my luck with being on the road.”

“I’m fine!” I told her. 

She looked at me and kept her gaze on my face. 

I looked at her back, waiting. 

“You have very beautiful eyes,” she said. 

“Thanks,” I replied. “Yours are nicer!” 

“Accept the compliment as is. You don’t always need to compliment back.”

I smiled. “Well, thank you.”

“I’m Charlotte,” she said. 

“I’m Bree,” I replied. 

We shook hands for a bit too long. But I didn’t want to let her go. I don’t think she did either.

“Well, you need a bandaid,” she said before pulling a small first aid kit from her bag. She placed it over my open wound and gently pressed it around against the skin. 

“Thanks,” I replied, a bit breathless. 

We stood there in silence. Then her gaze fixated on a part of my blouse. “What’s that?” she asked.

Oreo crumbs were on my shirt. “Oh, just a part of my donut. Probably slammed it into me when I fell.”

She smiled. “Let me buy you a new one.” 

She extended her hand towards me. I took it, and together, we went back to get some more donuts.
