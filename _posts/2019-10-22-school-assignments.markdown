“You know, Donna in Mamma Mia 2 had it totally right when she said she didn’t know how to sing about love when she wasn’t feeling it. I’m not feeling the writing of this stupid poem,” I told Cynthia as I sat on her floor in her room. We had to write a poem for class. My go-to topic was love. 

But today just felt like bullshit. 

Today felt like if Pretty Woman ended before Richard Gere went after Julia Roberts. 

Today felt like when Sally was at the New Year’s Eve party, miserable and alone and without a hope that Harry would show up because she was done with him. She was done with the friendship, done with trying to make things work.

Today, I saw the world for the realistic and cold place that it was. I didn’t believe in love. I didn’t believe in long term companionship. I only believed in what I could do for myself. What I could provide for myself. 

And the best I felt I could do for me was to not submit myself to the same goddamn broken heartache I saw three of my friends go through this week. 

“Then do what her friends tell her to do and write about what you do know,” Cynthia replied.

“That what I’ve believed all my life is stupid?”

“Sure. Just get it done and so you don’t worry about this assignment later on.” 

“Fine.”
