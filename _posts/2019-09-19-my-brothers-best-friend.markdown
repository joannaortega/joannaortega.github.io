My brother, Julian, was captain of the football team, so he was always surrounded by his football friends both at home and at school. He was quite charismatic, so everyone that met him loved him even if they didn’t want to. He just had that kind of personality that made you want to be around him. And honestly? He was such a good brother. Julian was kind to me and protective, even though I knew it was annoying to have a younger sister in your high school and at home. But he never indicated so. He would be welcoming when I approached his table during lunch and when I would run to steal snacks to take back to my room when he had movie nights and sleepovers at home with his friends. 

One friend in particular was always around: Phillip. He was a nice guy and I liked him. He was friends with my brother since they were in elementary school, and they grew up together. He was a warmer contrast to my brother. Whereas my brother and I shared the same black hair and blue eyes, Phillip’s eyes were hazel, between brown and green depending on the light he stood in. His hair was a light brown, almost reddish in some light. He was really an adorable creature. Phillip grew up around him--around us, I should say. He was one of the few that truly stuck around through thick and thin with Julian and they lived at each other’s homes. 

Which was clearly demonstrated as he walked right into my house and sat down next to me. 

“Hey, Julissa,” Phillip said as he walked in. 

I tried to keep my eyes glued to my television, but I offered him some popcorn I had in a bowl. “Sup. Julian is at football practice.”
 
“Yeah, I know.” He spoke kind of slowly. 

“He should be back soon,” I said when he looked at his hands. He seemed very nervous. He was wearing a nicer shirt than usual, kind of a green dress shirt that brought out his eyes. “Do you guys have any plans today?” I asked. 

“Well...yeah, but that’s not why I’m here.”

I laughed. “You don’t usually need a reason to be here, Phillip.”

He lowered his face into his palms, his elbows resting on his knees. “God. I suck at this,” I think he whispered, but I couldn’t be too sure.

“Out with it, home skillet.” 

“I like you,” he said, his face flushed and red.

“You’re good people. I like you too,” I replied to him before taking a fistfull of popcorn and shoving it into my mouth.

“No. I LIKE you, LIKE you. I want to take you out. Like on a date”

And I choked.

I started coughing and shoved the plate out of my lap. I couldn’t breathe. Phillip pounded on my back, until I spat everything on the floor.  

We both stayed silent when I started breathing again.

“So...do you want to go out?” Phillip asked me.

“Where did this come from?”

“Oh, I’ve liked you since we met. I thought you were pretty then. I still think so now. You’re very smart. I am rambling now. I will shut up.”

I sighed. “Oh, Phillip.” I guess he was good looking.

“Wanna go get ice cream?” he asked. “You probably can’t choke on that.”

I thought about it for a second. “Fine. But you’re telling Julian.”

“What am I telling Julian?” 

“Nevermind. Let's go.” 

He extended his hand and I took it, intertwining our fingers together.
