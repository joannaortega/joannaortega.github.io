It was supposed to be a quick flight. The band was flying in to do a show in Austin. I sat next to Benji and across from Oliver and Julian. We were thankfully riding first class. I hated crowds and people hovering over all of my personal space. Don’t get me wrong; I loved playing concerts, and I loved meeting fans. I loved organized lines to shake hands and hug and talk to people. But all too often, they surrounded us, without a chance of escaping. I felt my anxiety begin to rise the closer I felt we were to land.  

The stewardess passed by and I asked her for some water. 

“Can you bring her a drink too? And top me off?” Benji asked the stewardess.

“Yes, sir!” she said and brought us our desired drinks.

“You okay, Arianna?” Benji asked as I shot the liquor straight down and chased it with water. 

“Yep. Same thing as always,” I said in hopes of reassuring him. 

“But you’re fine being here on the plane, right?” he asked.

I nodded and smiled. “I don’t have a problem flying.”

Oliver turned to me. “Let’s look at the set list for this location.”

“New album exclusively?” Julian asked.

Oliver shook his head. “Nah. The more popular songs of the new album but I know that the fans will want to hear ‘A Brand New Life”, “Unknown Emotions”, and “Rage”.

“Yeah! Sounds good. Maybe “Rage” for encore?” I asked him. “It’s like popular enough that they will be satisfied but not so much that it would elicit some other song request.”

“I agree with that,” Benji said in support. 

Julian nodded. 

Oliver considered it and agreed. “It’s very fun to play.”

We discussed the order of the songs where we alternated some old and new songs. I was very excited, because I never figured that I would ever get this. That, we would ever make it big. Oliver was the lead singer, Julian played drums, Benji played bass or keyboard if need be, and I was the electric--and sometimes, acoustic--guitar player. From when I was five and even after I was a broody teenager, I would sit in my room and just play all the different types of music, in particular rock. Dad liked classic rock music. He was particularly supportive when I played Queen or the Beatles or Nirvana. My first concert with him was to see Foo Fighters when I was waaaay too young. But I felt like a fish in water among the crowd. So it’s what I knew and I looked up to. And I got great at playing. It was hard not to; I played every afternoon after I was done with homework or...a relationship. I felt more natural with my guitar on me than without.

Which is how I met the rest of the guys. I went to high school with my guitar once. Benji told me they needed a guitarist for their band. We started to hang out and have band practice week after week. We started booking gigs, and then started to become more and more popular. And now, here we were, in first class and with good mutual company. The four of us were a family away from family. Julian and Oliver had their respective partners. Benji and I were happily single. But we didn’t feel envious of what the other had.

Because Benji and I were single, we had time to know each other, and he knew this was one of my nightmare situations. As the plane began to descend, I felt the wave of nausea hit me like a ton of bricks. Benji looked at me and all I truly wanted was to have my guitar with me. I felt more comfortable strumming. Or invisible. 

He handed me a scrunchie he held for me around his wrist. While we landed, I began to tie my hair into a bun. He then handed me a baseball cap. I put it on and pulled some glasses from my purse. We finally landed, and Benji helped me get my carry-on out from the overhead compartment. 

“Are you ready?” Benji asked me.

“Sure. Ish. Maybe there won’t be a crowd.”

As we were checking into the airport from the flight and walking towards the area with our limo, I stopped dead in my tracks. I heard the high volume of people, singing along to our music. It was beautiful and terrifying. I was grateful for their dedication, but it was a lot.  

“Hey, can we get security to escort us to the car?” I asked Benjamin.

“Yeah. Sure.” He walked over to talk to one of the flight attendants and security personnel. He came back and told me they were going to start getting people to escort us to our car.

“Want us to go out there and see how bad it is?” Julian asked.

“Ehh.. no. Not unless you want to,” I replied. 

“Hey, we aren’t afraid to just go. But we can wait for you.”

“No, no, go ahead, if you want to.” 

Benjamin wrapped his arm around me, reassuringly. “Y’all can go find the car. I’ll stay here.”

I saw as Oliver and Julian walked away. I heard the loudness of the singing people, clearly loving our songs. But it made me insanely nervous. When the escorts finally arrived, they looked at us and decided to enclose us. 

“It’s a lot of people out there, okay?” one of them said. “We will surround you, but do your best to stay inside.”

I took a deep breath and I linked arms with Benjamin, who also put on some glasses at this point. I tried not to glance at the people or look suspicious, but people were all behind a railing. When they saw us, they decided to jump the railing to get to us. They pushed us around, snapping pictures and trying to take selfies with us. Benjamin tightened his grip, painfully so, but I was grateful. Someone pulled off my hat and I couldn’t see who but my hair also fell from the unsecured bun. 

Get me out of here, I just kept repeating, shutting my eyes and walking in the direction Benjamin pulled me. I loved the fans. They were mostly harmless but this was...a lot. I just needed space. 

And suddenly I was pushed into a car. I crawled inside and Oliver quickly pulled me in as Benjamin closed the door behind us. The limo immediately started to pull away and as much as I loved my life and creating art for a living, I hated being in large crowds so much. And five years later, I was still afraid.
