“Hey, so I’m going to go shopping! Wanna tag along?” Iris asked me when she called. 

“You already know the answer. I’ll be ready in thirty!”

“Perfect!” she said and hung up. 

I ran to my closet to find clothes to put on and get my makeup done. 

In a past life, I was probably a clothing designer. Or a professional shopper. Some of my friends may claim I am a professional shopper now. But honestly? I just really love clothes. I love the way it fits my body. I love to feel for the texture, especially here in Texas. Some textures are so cool weatherwise, like polyester in specific brands. I like the softness of a cotton camisole and some basketball shorts to sleep in. I like the warmth of a wool and the laziness of a good sweater and leggings.I liked clothes that could make me cool in the summer and warm in the winter. And I like the way some things fit and sit on my curves. I could be...whoever I wanted to be with the versatility of clothes.

And today, I was feeling some jeggings with some little booties and a polyester floral top. I loved and had way too many floral tops and clothing. But I loved them.  Roses and peonies and other types of flowers that I had admired were now against my skin, and I looked livelier. So I decided to keep loving life and embracing it to me. And it seemed stupid to try to base life, beauty, and goodness on clothes. But there was something magical about clothes. Just as they can breathe life, there is a way they can depict other things. It is wearable art. In that sense, I feel like an artist curating pieces of art for my own closet. 

I applied some more pink-rosy eyeshadow and some red on the outer corners and white on the inner corners. I loved the way eyeshadow could tie the self to the clothes. I used a dusty rose lipstick on my lips. After slipping into my flats, I was ready to go.  

Luckily, I got a text from Iris. 

>Iris: On my way!

>Me: Drive safe!

If I found something that matched me and I loved, I would go for it and buy it. I had enough clothes in my closet to knock anyone out and not wear the same thing every weekend if I chose to for a very long time. But if there was something that I loved, I like indulging myself, especially with things that make me feel pretty. 

Clothes can manipulate and influence the self esteem. They raised me up and made me feel beautiful. And when I was more insecure, I tried them on and took them off after if they didn’t work their magic. And if I kept them on because of lack of time to start anew or just plain desire, I would decide to just roll with the punches and accept things as they were. They were like symbolism in a book; they hid meaning for me. And I knew what they meant to me. I just wondered if the rest of the world knew to interpret for themselves based on what I wore. I wondered if people knew I felt light and pretty. I wonder if they knew I felt badass. 

I grabbed my purse and stepped outside of my house, locking the door behind me. Iris didn’t live too far away and as I stepped into the driveway, I saw her car pulled up to my street. I half ran to the passenger’s side and got in. 

“Hey, Lyla!” she said to me. “You look so beautiful!”

“Thanks! So do you!” And I meant it. She was wearing a black tank with a rainbow skirt and some wedges. Iris totally got what I was talking about.

“Anything in particular that you’re looking for?” she asked.

I smiled. “Nope, if I find something, I’ll get it. If not, that’s fine.”

“I need a cute dress shirt,” she said, “I have a job interview.”

“Then we need to find you the most kick ass top that will give you so much confidence and get you that job!”

She laughed. She drove us to the nearest mall where we began our hunt. And as we walked in, I realized that perhaps I romanticize attire too much. Perhaps I give it too much importance. But truly, they are quite lovely things to decorate who we are. Just as we do, they can either provide a statement or not. I guess the beauty in it is that clothes aren’t just clothes.
