I was never an agile person. I knew what my strengths and weaknesses were. I was lanky and unathletic. I was also highly anemic most of the time, so as much as I tried to keep it under control, a small slip would render me breathless and dizzy. It didn’t scare me as much anymore. I knew what it was; I knew my illness in and out. I knew at the onset of a headache that my iron pills would help me out. 

I was happy to no longer need the B-12 vitamins all that often. When we first discovered my little funky illness, I needed to get like relatively daily shots. Now it’s just a shot every once in a while, specifically once a month. Maybe I shouldn’t complain too much. This isn’t truly forever, just long enough until I manage it. Fortunately for me, it made a great topic to talk about regarding my college essays. But unfortunately for me, I was the guy that passed out a couple of times in high school. 

I wasn’t driving very much either these days. We didn’t want to risk me getting behind the wheel and then having a repercussion of my illness just here to mess me up while I was traveling, or anyone else really. So I also had to deal with my mom driving me everywhere. My friends didn’t mind but other people had comments they liked to throw around when I was in earshot. 

As I watched the clock to let us go, I made eye contact with Alice, a girl I’d shared classes with since I was in middle school. I liked her once upon a time but I was never one of those people that was overwhelmed by emotions. And she was a very emotional being and it was clear when she liked someone. 

The bell rang. I got up in unison with everyone else. The crowds started heading out towards the back. I started heading out with them, happy to be out for the weekend. Crap. I left my book in my locker. I turned around quickly to head back in the direction of my locker and I felt dizzy immediately. I staggered to the side of the hallway, taking slow steps against the traffic of the incoming people. When I felt the nausea build up, I closed my eyes and stood next to the collection of lockers. 

“Do you need help?” I heard a voice ask.

I opened my eyes to see Alice standing there in front of me. “Yeah, I’m fine. Just need to get to my locker.”

She looked at me. “Do you need help getting there?”

“Ahh. No.” I didn’t sound convincing but when I started to move, she walked next to me. 

“Thanks, Alice,” I whispered.

She just nodded and stood next to me when I got to my locker. I pulled out my book and she helped me put it in my backpack. 

“Anemia is really fucking hard, isn’t it?” she asked.

“Yeah. It’s just like...I feel like I’m delicate and depending on literally everyone.”

“Well, if it helps, I think you’re stronger than anyone I’ve ever known. This sure as heck slows you down.”

“Yeah, it does. But I don’t think I’ve ever been a fast mover to begin with.” 

“I guess not, but you’ve been a very thoughtful person. I think you’ve moved by being in your own head for a while.”

“Maybe so,” I mused. 

We started moving back out of the school. 

“Please take care of yourself, Jacob,” she said when we got to the front of a school and I saw my mom’s car.

“You too, Alice.” 

She kissed my cheek before walking away.

I felt lightheaded for a different reason as I walked into my mom’s car.

“Good day at school?” Mom asked.

“I guess so,” I said and smiled as she drove away.
