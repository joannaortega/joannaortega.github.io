My phone buzzed, at around 9:30 am everyday. I knew who it was without looking. She knew I had class, or work. She knew I was fine, but she had this need to always check in. She always needed confirmation that I was alive and okay. 

    Mom: Good morning, honey!

I paused for a second and decided to send the answers to her questions in one text. 
		
    Me: In class. I’m fine. I already ate. 

    Mom: Okay, honey!

I loved her so much. But I needed space. I needed time to myself. I needed time to learn who I was. And even though it was only truly a couple of messages in a day, I felt smothered. And I felt guilty for wanting some more space. I felt guilty for not wanting to talk all the time. Because I knew that the day that she was no longer with me, there would be eons of regret. Then, I’d want to talk to her. Then, I’d want her to tell me that she loves me, and I’d want to tell her that she means the world to me for all the sacrifices she made for me and my brother. 

But for now, I feel so selfish. It wasn’t that I didn’t miss her. I just wanted to be left alone. I needed to learn how to be a grown up. I wanted to be independent. Because that day, the day she wouldn’t be there would come someday, and I needed to know that I would be able to survive on my own. Because let’s face it, I had been spoonfed all of my life. I needed to know who I was and what I needed to become in order to survive in the daily world. I especially NEEDED, just desperately needed, to become who I wanted to be. 

The thing is, my mother loved so much. That’s where I got it from. I thought I loved a lot, but I think the way how she felt about me and my siblings surpassed anyone else’s potential to love. She had the greatest of the positive emotions. I wished I was more like her in that sense. I had the firecracker spirit that swung both low and high. She bawled her eyes out when my brother left for college and then when I did. 

Mom cried after visiting all the time, just as grandma did over her. And she loved so much. And she loved ME so much. And I loved so much. And I loved HER so much. But I couldn’t bring myself to exert that much emotion right now, even though I knew that I would probably be that way towards my own children years from now--if I eventually chose to have children of my own. For that reason, I didn’t deserve that love--or at least I felt I didn’t deserve that much affection.  

And before the guilty feelings could swallow me whole because I knew that other people had parents who didn’t love them enough, I pulled out my phone to send out a text: 

    Me: Mom, I love you. I’ll call you after class at the end of the day

A second later:

    Mom: :)

I knew that would make her day.

My professor dismissed class. I got up and went to my next class.
