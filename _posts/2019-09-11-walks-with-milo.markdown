Every morning and every evening, I took Milo out for a walk. He was a very active German Shepherd and standing on his hind legs, he was definitely taller than my 5’4 height. He was a friendly pile of fluff. So before I had to work, I took him out with me for my morning jog to help him get some of that doggie energy out before I had to start work in my home office. He had an open yard with a doggie door to play in and out as he pleased. But I wanted him to be healthy and happy. 

And part of his doggie happiness was his little girlfriend, Lucky. She was very much like him, a German Shepherd with a black snout and a tan hair. She lived at the end of the road. I knew the owner, Charlie; he was a nice neighbor. We went to each other’s barbeques so our dogs could interact some more. They were both still relatively young, albeit already big in size. But Lucky and Milo loved to see each other all the time, so we tried our best to allow them time to interact with one another, sometimes twice a day, but at the bare minimum, once. Sometimes Charlie left Lucky with me when he had things to do during the day and sometimes I left Milo with him when I had to go into meetings with other people. 

So here we were, Milo ahead of me, knowing where he was going. He knew how to get to Lucky and Charlie. I jogged a little bit faster as Milo pulled me. Lucky was hopping against the gate in the front of Charlie’s house. As we got closer, I noticed Charlie was outside. He was sitting on the steps that lead up to his home.

“Hey!” I said enthusiastically. It had been a good morning so far. My bouts of insomnia hadn’t gotten to me last night and I got a great night’s rest. 

He didn’t seem to share my luck. He looked...stressed. “Hey, Kate. So we need to talk.”

I stopped dead in my tracks and looked at him. “Oh no. What’s wrong?”

“I took Lucky to the vet yesterday.”

Oh no. I opened the gate and allowed Milo to run towards Lucky, letting go of the leash. I ran to sit down next to him. She looked fine. Milo and her were just happily roaming around his yard.

“Oh god. Is she okay?”

“She’s pregnant.” He looked so tired. 

“Oh.” I pursed my lips and blurted the following out: “I’m too young to be a grandmother.”  

 He looked at me. “It’s not funny.”

“What’s the problem?” I asked him.

“It’s just a very bad time right now,” he said. “I have a lot of things going on at work. It’s just a lot going on.” 
  
“Hey, Milo is the father. I will be a responsible grandma. If you need help and need me to take care of Lucky more days, please let me know.”

“Thanks, Kate.” He gave me a small smile.

“You have my number,” I said as I patted him on the shoulder. I got up. “Come on, Milo! It’s time for work!” 

Milo ran to me. I picked up his dragging leash. We ran back home. 

---

When Milo and I went for our walk in the afternoon, I brought a bag of kibble and snacks with a note that said, CHILD SUPPORT. I left it in his front porch because neither him nor Lucky were home.
