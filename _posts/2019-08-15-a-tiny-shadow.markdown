Everyday for lunch, Linda went outside to eat. There was a terrace with umbrellas to block from the rain and intense sun. There were also phone charging docks and fans near the tables. She took late lunches because she always had to wait until her boss got back from lunch, and her boss ate at the same time as everyone else. So she got to avoid the crowds and became the odd one out in the office, usually with a chance to sit outside alone. Luckily for her, she didn’t mind being alone. She got to enjoy the scenery in peace in peace. There were flowers and trees and wildlife loved to spend time around the area.  

On this particular day, as Linda sat and ate her salmon and wild rice for lunch, she looked at the general scenery again. She looked around and noticed the apparent crumbs at the tables around her. But before she could get annoyed by the apparent littering and carelessness of her coworkers, she noticed how the birds would fly in and nibble at the crumbs. Sometimes they would successfully pick up the crumbs, sometimes not. But today, she was startled by how quickly they all flew away from the tables. She turned around to see the cause of the sudden behavior and saw a tiny black kitten with its tiny paws, roaming around, meowing loudly.

Linda looked at the kitten who was licking at the tables. She tossed some salmon in the kitten’s direction. She watched as the hungry kitten devoured the entire piece in seconds. Linda ate the last remnants of her food before putting her plastic container on the ground away from herself and dumping the rest of her water into the container. 

The kitten approached slowly. When she noticed Linda wasn’t moving towards her, she scurried towards the water and began to drink. When the kitten scampered away, Linda picked up the dish, threw it away and went back inside to continue walking. 

---

The next day, she realized the cat was around again, so she threw some rice on the ground and allowed it some of her water. She finally decided to pay attention to the kitten. It was quite thin and meager but most of all sad and dirty. Maybe the kitten was lost. 

She tried to coax it towards her, but the cat ran away when she stood up. 

It must have a home, Linda thought to herself to appease her concerned mind. So she calmly went to work. 

---

Work ran late that evening, so when she got out, it was dark. It was also pouring outside. She heard a faint meow from the distance. She opened her umbrella and looked around. She saw the kitten stuck between a ledge between the street and the fountain. The snout was slowly becoming submerged. Her eyes began to well up. She ran to the kitten and pulled the tiny creature up carefully, taking her sweater and wrapping it around the kitten for warmth. She ran to the car and turned up the heater, placing the kitten in an empty container and securing it with a seatbelt. The kitten began to meow louder after a while. 

When they got to her apartment, she took the kitten, who she determined as a male, straight to the sink in her bathroom. She scrubbed him down with shampoo and rinsed him off. He allowed himself to be cleaned and was slowly and warmly blowdried. Afterwards, Linda cooked both of them some chicken, his without seasoning. They ate together on her kitchen counter, and she fixed him up a bed in her bedroom near the door. He started to settle down in his new bed but after noticing she still wasn’t going to bed just yet, he got up and followed her around. 

He sat on the toilet as she showered and brushed her teeth. He followed her as she put her lunch for the next day in a container and in her fridge. And when it came time for them to actually go to bed, he plopped down and snuggled around her face in the pillow next to her.

She scratched him on his head. “We can go get you more stuff tomorrow. But for now, good night, Shadow.” 

She turned off the lamp next to her bed, and they both went to sleep.
